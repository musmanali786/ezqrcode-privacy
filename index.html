1. What we collect

Information you provide

  • Account information — if you choose to create an account, we collect your email address and (for password sign-ups) a securely hashed password. If you sign in with Google, we receive your email and Google account ID via OAuth.
  • QR content you generate or save — the URLs, text, contact details, Wi-Fi credentials, payment usernames, and any titles, captions, or logos you attach to a QR code.

Information collected automatically

  • App activity — counts of QR codes you generate, save, share, or upload to cloud, used to enforce per-day free-tier limits. For guest users this is stored only on your device; for signed-in users this also lives in our backend account record.
  • Device & advertising data — Google AdMob collects an advertising identifier and limited device metadata to serve ads. See AdMob's data-disclosure guide.

What we do NOT collect: we do not collect your contacts, photos library, microphone audio, precise location, SMS, call logs, browsing history, or any health/financial data.

2. How we use your information

  • To generate, save, sync, and display QR codes you create.
  • To enforce free-tier daily and lifetime limits fairly across users.
  • To authenticate you when you sign in and keep your session secure.
  • To serve and measure advertising via Google AdMob.
  • To respond to support requests you send us by email.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use your QR content for advertising targeting.

3. Third parties we share data with

  • Supabase — our backend hosting and authentication provider. Stores your account record (email, hashed credentials, sign-in metadata) and any QR codes you choose to upload to cloud. See Supabase Privacy Policy.
  • Google (Sign-In + AdMob) — handles Google OAuth sign-in and serves advertisements inside the app. See Google Privacy Policy.

All data transmitted between your device and these services is encrypted in transit using HTTPS/TLS.

4. Security & retention

  • All network traffic is encrypted (HTTPS/TLS).
  • Passwords are stored hashed by Supabase Auth — we never see them in plaintext.
  • QR codes you save without signing in stay only on your device.
  • Cloud-synced QR codes are retained until you delete them, or until you delete your account.
  • Backups of cloud data may persist for up to 30 days after deletion before being permanently erased.

5. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you (email us).
  • Correct inaccurate information (edit it in the app or email us).
  • Delete your data — see the sections below.
  • Withdraw consent at any time by deleting the app and your account.

6. How to delete your account

You can permanently delete your EZ QR Code account, your saved QR codes, and all associated data either from inside the app or by emailing us.

Option A — Delete from inside the app (fastest)

  1. Open EZ QR Code on your device.
  2. Tap the Profile tab.
  3. Scroll down and tap Delete account.
  4. Confirm the action.

Your account, email, sign-in records, and all cloud-synced QR codes are removed immediately. Local QR codes saved on your device are also cleared.

Option B — Email request

If you can't access the app, send an email to m.usmanali@threados.ai from the email address linked to your account, with the subject line "Delete my account". We will process the deletion within 7 working days and confirm by reply.

What gets deleted

  • Your account record (email, hashed password, OAuth identifier)
  • All QR codes you uploaded to cloud
  • Your usage counters and preferences

What is kept (and for how long)

  • Encrypted backups may retain your data for up to 30 days before permanent erasure.
  • Aggregated, non-identifying usage statistics (e.g. "100 QR codes were created today" with no link to you) are kept indefinitely.

7. How to delete specific data without deleting your account

You don't have to delete your entire account to remove individual items.

Delete individual QR codes

  1. Open EZ QR Code.
  2. Tap the Library tab.
  3. Find the QR code you want to remove and tap the trash icon on that row.
  4. Confirm. The QR code is removed from both your device and (if you were signed in) our backend.

Clear all local-only data

To clear every QR code stored locally on your device without deleting your account:

  1. Android Settings → Apps → EZ QR Code → Storage → Clear data.

Email request

For any other data deletion request, email m.usmanali@threados.ai with the subject "Delete specific data" and describe what you want removed. We will process it within 7 working days.

8. Children's privacy

EZ QR Code is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn that we have collected such information, we will delete it.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the latest version. Material changes will be communicated in the app or by email if you have an account.
